Habits that do not allow you to break with poverty.

Habits that do not allow you to break with poverty.

In this list, I have collected habits that cause a person to live worse than they could if they had the same income level. Of course, how to live is everyone's business, the only sad thing is these habits they are inherited as an example of behavior. And inherited habits are already much more difficult to change.

1. The very first habit is to constantly borrow money. Sometimes it happens that you have to ask for a loan, but when it is a system and a constant behavior, it is a habit. By and large, the difference between the poor and the poor secured consists of "debit and credit", it is both financial + and - . The borrower always pays more. The only way out is a strong-willed decision to pay your debts and not take any more.

2. The habit of not planned spending. For some people, all their money is one sum of which they spend. As a result, in one month they they spend more on food, but not enough on clothes. In another month, they buy clothes and then borrow for food. On the third day, they pay their rent debts. As a result, they constantly come to point 1mu â€“ the need to borrow money.

3. the Habit of making spontaneous purchases, on the principle of "I want and I need". Periodically we all need something I want and it is normal to buy what I want. But when this happens every month and blows a hole in the budget â€“ this is a problem. Most often, this indicates problems in life, which leads to dissatisfaction with life. Spontaneous purchases give a feeling of euphoria and satisfaction, but turn out to be even greater dissatisfaction in the following days after purchases.

4. the Habit of buying status items not based on status. In General, there are statistics that most of the expensive things are bought by those who can not constantly afford it. In this case, the status item it makes you feel better and more important. Such purchases take out a lot of money, so you have to save on the rest. But unfortunately, they do not add much to the status, since one thing cannot correct the overall impression. It is better to have things of the same price range.

"I'm not interested in cars and I don't want others to envy me. Don't confuse cost and quality of life." Warren Buffett is a billionaire investor.

5. The habit of buying fashion items to be in trend and it concerns not only clothes. Such things are usually sold with the maximum amount of hype at the very beginning. This habit is peculiar to young people. If with age it does not pass this suggests that it, as well as the previous one, have roots in low self-esteem.

All these habits unbalance the budget by pulling money out of the wallet in one direction to the detriment of others. As already mentioned, the only difference is whether you live by debit or credit. All these habits drag a person on credit.
