It happened. The S&P500 has taken the bar at 3000 points.

It would be appropriate to use the expression: it has never happened before, and here it is again. The S&P500 has never been at the 3000 level, but the achievement this bar was quite predictable. Despite fears, fears and prophecies of a crisis or prolonged depression. Despite the correction of the end of 2018 , this record has been broken.

Those who were not afraid to enter the recession for six months would earn 15-20%% in dollars. I have clients that I've been making up for the portfolio is in this period of General fear. There are clients who had portfolios before, but are actively buying the shares at the time of the downturn - they are now rewarded for their patience.

But now what? Invest or wait for the collapse? What should I do now? My decision is to continue investing and actively buy up shares. First of all-following the precepts of Buffett: to be greedy when others are afraid. And people, banks, funds, managers-they are very afraid.

We are told of crisis after crisis. First in 2018, then in 2019. There is still "hope" for a collapse, but expectations have already shifted towards 2020. Let them be afraid. This does not mean that but I'm right. I am well aware that a collapse or correction can occur at any time - tomorrow, the day after tomorrow, in a month or in 5 years. It's just unpredictable. I'm just following my clear strategy.

When deciding whether to invest now, think about what might happen. If you enter the market and there is no collapse so, you will be able to earn a good dollar income.

But if you do not enter the market and there is no collapse - you are in a difficult situation. Because with each new growth of the market to enter will be more and more scary. If the market falls - Yes, you are in the chocolate. But even if you are already in the market in the event of a collapse you can make money on this. Dividends, airbags, etc. - will do their job.

For me, reaching the 3,000-point milestone for the S&P500 doesn't affect my strategy in any way. This does not affect the quality of companies, nor does it affect profits or dividends. Yes, this makes you choose carefully not to buy overpriced companies, but that's all.
