ðŸ”ŠWhat will happen to FINEX FXUS in a CRISIS?ðŸ§¨

Friends, today in the category "Million from zero" we will discuss: what to do with FXUS in the event of a crisis, when the stock market and the price of an ETF unit fall-fall-fall...

First and foremost, I don't I recommend trying to sell shares at the beginning of the crisis. I have already covered this topic and the main reason why I do not recommend it is the inability to guess when exactly this "beginning", when exactly this "end"... This means that there is an extremely high probability of losing real money on speculations of this kind. If you are investing in an fxus ETF as part of a strategy "A million from zero", then nothing should change for you. Well, the share price will drop by 50% - you will be able to buy not 1, but 2 shares per month.

I should also note that even if all the owners of FXUS units rush to sell them off, this in itself should not lead to a serious decline in the price. Requests for sale are met by the market maker. What about you the value of units is technically linked to the dynamics of the index, which in turn is based on the value of assets included in its composition.

Yes, if there is a global collapse in the stock market, then the value of assets can fall, and this will entail a fall in both the index and the unit. And - as often happens in a crisis, the nominal price can become even lower than the cost of the companies ' real business. This is a great reason to use the airbag, which you should have, among other things, according to the "Million from zero"strategy.

As for closing the Fund, this may happen in the event of a global exit of investors from the Fund the Fund's assets are in a panic. Assets under management will remain low, the Commission will not be sufficient to cover transaction costs, and the Fund may be liquidated. In this case, the assets are simply sold off at the market price, and the money is returned to the investors in proportion to their investment. Of course, when selling on a falling market in the market, you will get a realized loss. But if you immediately invest your money in other stocks-also at low prices, then I do not see anything terrible in this.

To sum up: investing in the "Million from scratch" strategy in a crisis is no different from investing in any other fundamental strategy. Keep it, buy it up, averaging and ignoring the noise.

Good luck with your investment!
