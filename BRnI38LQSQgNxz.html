Impact of the employment market on economic indicators

Impact of the employment market on economic indicators

The employment market is one of the main indicators of economic growth. The economy is a complex system, all the elements of which are in a certain relationship with each other.Â  Thus, the situation in the labor market it can have a strong impact on other elements of the economy and, in General, be a significant impetus for changing activity on the stock exchange.

The labor market includes several key indicators. In most countries, the unemployment rate is the key one. The unemployment rate (eng. unemployment rate shows the ratio of the number of unemployed to the total number of working population. It is obvious that high unemployment rates negatively affect the country's economy. Its effect will be a direct decrease in the level of consumption of durable goods, as well as a decline in the level of consumption of non-durable goods productions.

In the United States, the main indicator of the employment market is the number of jobs created during the month in the non-agricultural sector, that is, in the business sector (English Nonfarm Payrolls). Providing more than 200,000 jobs will be considered a good indicator. This situation is on the the employment market will have a positive impact on the growth of the dollar. In order to understand what indicator we should expect in the next news report, we need to familiarize ourselves with such a phenomenon as Jobless claims .

Every week in the report in economic news, an indicator of the number of applications for unemployment benefits, or Jobless claims, is released. The number of more than 325-350 thousand primary requests is considered negative. Accordingly, if negative jobless claims figures are released during the month, then at the moment of nonfarm Payrolls data release (every first Friday of a new month month) we should not expect positive results.

When calculating the unemployment rate in the United States, repeated applications for benefits are not taken into account, so the nonfarm Payrolls indicators are considered more accurate when calculating the real state of Affairs in the employment market.
